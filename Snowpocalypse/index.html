❄️ Snowpocalypse ❄️

A Comedy About Survival, Snow, and Snacks
Written by Gentry Conner, Reesa Devers, and Elizabeth Unger

About the Play

If you've lived in Winston-Salem for more than five minutes, as a student, resident, or other, you know the drill: the local news mentions a "flurry," and suddenly the bread and milk aisles at Whole Foods and Publix look like a scene from an apocalypse movie. In Snowpocalypse, we take that relatable North Carolina chaos and dial it up…a lot.

While our characters are busy arguing over Hot Cheetos and Wii Basketball, the reality of Winston-Salem winters can be brutal. Forsyth County often deals with travel chaos, where freezing rain turns Business 40 (also known as Salem Parkway) into a literal skating rink. In real life, the City of Winston-Salem deploys over 100 pieces of equipment to clear 1,000 miles of road, but even then, the "slippery streets" mentioned in the Winston-Salem Chronicle remind us that sometimes, the safest place to be is inside bundled up.

This play highlights the hilarious divide between those who are prepared for the weather and those who aren't… whether you're a Beatrice, meticulously packing a splint, or a Chad, thinking a 12-pack of Coors is an essential supply. So, as you watch the "Snowpocalypse" unfold on stage, remember the Newscaster's warning: enjoy the calm, but stay ready. You never know when you might need to pick a lock with a paperclip just to get to your favorite energy drink!

Synopsis

When a massive winter storm threatens Winston-Salem, two neighboring college apartments prepare in very different ways. On one side, Beatrice, Veronica, and Ashleigh stock up with military precision—first aid kits, emergency supplies, and enough provisions to survive a month-long siege. Next door, Chad, Brad, and Vlad's idea of preparation involves beer, energy drinks, and faith that everything will work out.

As the storm hits, things take an unexpected turn when the boys get locked out of their apartment. With the temperature dropping and their precious beer stuck inside, they're forced to seek help from their overprepared neighbors. Together, this mismatched group must navigate frozen pizza debates, Wii Basketball obsessions, and the age-old question: can you really pick a lock with a paperclip?

But just when they think they've survived the storm, the news delivers a chilling warning: another, bigger storm is on its way. Round two is about to begin!
